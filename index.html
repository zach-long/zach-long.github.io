About

I graduated May 2016 from Messiah College with a BM in Music Performance, which might seem strange to read on a web development portfolio. Though I spent my years at Messiah playing bass trombone with countless groups, during my senior year I decided to begin pursuing a previously unexplored but long-time interest of mine, programming. For as long as I can remember I have been drawn to technology and have had an intense curiosity for learning why and how things work. The reason I enjoy coding is ultimately the same reason I have always enjoyed music - I am amazed by how small, seemingly insignificant individual components can come together to form something incredible and something that is much greater than the sum of its parts.

In college I discovered I have a great passion for driving improvement, whether it be making positive change in my own life, inspiring someone else to grow, or changing the world around me for the better. I am drawn into the realm of technology and web development because I derive great joy in being able to design a tool, whether a website or an app, that helps someone achieve a goal they are striving for.

My design philosophy could ironically be coined “extravagant minimalism”, I want to create something that is clean and functional, with enough ‘flair’ to make it easy on the eyes but not so much frivolity that it impedes function. In development and design, as in art, I strongly believe every element should have a specific purpose. In writing code I firmly believe that it should always be clean, easy to comprehend, and easy to come back to. I also have a penchant for work that is simply done correctly. A snippet of code, section of a webpage, application, or project should never be “good enough” or have a hacked-in, unnecessarily hard-coded solution.
